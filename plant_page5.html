About these Plants

These plants thrive in wet, water-retentive clay soils under full sunlight. They include water-loving species, wetland grasses, and certain flowering plants that can tolerate occasional flooding. Clay soil holds nutrients and moisture well, which these plants utilize to grow densely and robustly.

02

Impacts on Parks

Introducing these species into Myanmar’s parks helps manage waterlogged areas, prevent soil erosion, and improve wetland habitats for birds and aquatic life. They are perfect for park ponds, lakesides, and low-lying areas that collect rainwater. In Myanmar, you can mostly find these plants along riverbanks, floodplains, and wetland regions such as the Ayeyarwady Delta and Inle Lake areas, where clay-rich soils and surface water are abundant.
