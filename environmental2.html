Environmental Zones
This section maps the park’s environmental layers—sunlight, shade, soil type, moisture, water access, and visitor paths—and matches them with suitable plants. It explains each plant’s needs and why they are the best fit for each environmental zone to support healthy, sustainable growth.
What is even GIS system?

Exploring the details about Environmental Layers

In Geographic Information Systems (GIS), environmental layers represent spatial datasets that describe various factors influencing ecosystems, land use, and habitat conditions.

For the factors you listed—sunlight, shade, soil type, moisture level, water access, and visitor footpaths—each can be conceptualized as a separate GIS layer. Below is a detailed breakdown.

☼Different Types of Sunlight

Full Sun

Gets 6+ hours of direct sunlight daily. Ideal for bright flowers, shrubs, and sun-loving herbs.

Part Shade / Partial Sun

Receives 3–6 hours of sunlight. Great for plants that enjoy some sun but need protection from intense heat.

Full Shade

Gets less than 3 hours of direct sun. Perfect for cool, low-light areas under trees or beside buildings.

Different Types of Soil

1
Sandy Soil

Gritty, drains fast, and warms quickly. It loses nutrients easily, so only drought-tolerant plants grow well unless enriched with organic matter.

2
Clay soil

Heavy, sticky, and slow-draining but nutrient-rich. It can compact easily, so plants must handle wet conditions and tougher root growth.

3
Loamy Soil

A balanced mix of sand, silt, and clay. Drains well, holds nutrients, and supports most plants—ideal for parks and gardens.

4
Silty Soil

Smooth and fine, holds moisture better than sand but drains more easily than clay. Fertile but can compact if stepped on too much.

Moisture Levels

Dry

Drains quickly and doesn’t hold much water. Only drought-resistant plants thrive, often with deep roots to reach moisture.

Moderate

Stays evenly moist without becoming soggy. Supports most common park plants and needs regular but not heavy watering.

Moist

Remains damp for long periods and suits plants that enjoy consistent hydration, often found in shaded or low areas.

Wet/Waterlogged

Soil stays soaked and drains very slowly. Only wetland or aquatic plants can survive here.

Ways to Access Water

Rain-fed

Plants rely solely on natural rainfall and must tolerate dry periods and seasonal changes.

Groundwater-fed

Plants get water from deep underground sources, so they often grow deep roots to reach stable moisture.

Irrigation-fed

Watered using sprinklers, hoses, or drip systems, allowing controlled watering for plants that need regular moisture.

Surface Water

Plants grow near ponds or streams where soil stays damp from nearby water, perfect for moisture-loving species.

Find Plants Suitable for Your Park

Cactus

Thrives in dry, sunny, sandy soil with minimal water.

Lavender

Prefers moderate sunlight, loamy soil, and regular watering.

Fern

Loves shaded, damp areas with loamy soil and groundwater access.

Water Lily

Grows in full sun, wet clay soil, and surface water.

Aloe Vera

Needs hot, dry sunlight and sandy soil; drought-resistant and low-maintenance.

Orchid

Thrives in partial sunlight, moist loamy soil, and groundwater-fed conditions.

Marigold

Requires full sun, moderate moisture, loamy soil, and regular watering.

Lotus

Grows in wet clay soil, shaded or partial sun, near surface water.

Papaya

Prefers full sun, moist loamy soil, and regular watering; common tropical fruit in Myanmar.

Basil

Partial sunlight, moderately moist loamy soil, rain-fed or light watering.

Chili

Full sun, loamy soil, moderate moisture; requires regular watering for good fruit.

Banana

Full sun, fertile loamy soil, moist conditions; often grown using groundwater irrigation.

Hibiscus

Thrives in full sun, sandy soil with moderate moisture; colorful tropical flower.

Staghorn Fern

Loves shaded areas with moist soil and consistent groundwater.

Jasmine

Partial sun, loamy soil, moderate watering; fragrant tropical flower commonly grown in Myanmar.

Pineapple

Full sun, sandy soil, drought-tolerant; rain-fed or occasional watering.

Water Spinach

Partial sun, wet clay soil, grows near water; popular leafy vegetable in Myanmar.

Mango

Full sun, loamy soil, moderately moist conditions; common fruit tree in tropical Myanmar.

Sweet Potato

Requires full sun, sandy soil, moderate moisture; rain-fed vegetable widely grown locally.

Rice

Full sun, wet clay soil, grown in paddies with surface water; staple crop in Myanmar.
