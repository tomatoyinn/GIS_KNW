01

About these Plants

These plants are suited for shaded or fully shaded areas with consistent moisture, often from groundwater or nearby streams. Ferns, mosses, and shade-tolerant shrubs are typical examples. They are adapted to low-light conditions and help improve air quality and humidity levels in shaded park areas.

02

Impacts on Parks

Using these plants in Myanmar's parks can transform under-tree areas, shaded pathways, and near ponds into lush, cool retreats. They stabilize soil, prevent erosion, and provide important microhabitats for small wildlife. You can often find these species in forested areas, along rivers, and in park regions with high groundwater levels, especially in Yangon’s botanical gardens and the forested hills of Kachin and Shan States.
